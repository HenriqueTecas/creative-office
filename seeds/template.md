# Seed: [Theme — keep it conceptual, not industry-specific]

## The Moment
[A specific frustrating/interesting/weird moment that a real person experiences. This anchors everything.]

Example: "It's 11pm and you just realized the airline charged you twice for a flight you cancelled three weeks ago. You don't have the energy to call them. You don't even know which number to call. You just want it fixed by morning."

Example: "A farmer in Alentejo checks her phone. It rained last night but her irrigation system ran anyway because the soil sensors in Zone 3 are miscalibrated. She's wasted 2,000 liters of water and she doesn't have time to fix it because the harvest crew arrives in an hour."

Example: "You're sitting at dinner and your friend describes an experience that sounds exactly like something you built in a brainstorm session six months ago. Someone else shipped it. You had the idea first but you never made it real."

## Domain
[Broad conceptual territory, NOT an industry vertical.

BAD: "AI agents for consumer advocacy in Europe"
GOOD: "Products that make invisible power dynamics visible and tradable"]

## Temporal Frame
[When does this exist? Buildable by what year? What technology is assumed?]

## Constraints
[Hard constraints that shape the output:
- Regulatory boundaries
- Technical feasibility
- Market reality
- What the first customer looks like]

## Provocation
[A statement or question that challenges a fundamental assumption.
Should make the Generator uncomfortable. Should not have an obvious answer.

Example: "The quantified self movement failed because it made the body into a dashboard. What if instead of reading your body like a spreadsheet, you could use your biological signals as an instrument?"]

## Anti-targets
[What output do we NOT want? Name the cliché version of this brainstorm.
- The boring startup that's "X but for Y"
- The obvious AI wrapper
- The incremental feature that already exists]

## Strangeness Seeds
[2-3 conceptual fragments from outside the domain that agents should consider cross-pollinating with the main theme. These are NOT ideas — they're lenses.]

Examples:
- "What if the product's users and its customers are different entities entirely?"
- "What if the product makes money by making itself unnecessary?"
- "What if the target market doesn't exist yet and the product creates it?"
- "Apply the logic of immune systems / futures market / mycelial networks / religious conversion / addiction / grief stages to this domain"
- "What would this look like if it were designed to be as uncomfortable as possible for VCs?"

---

## Seed Writing Guidelines

### DO:
- Start with a concrete moment (The Moment)
- Name the invisible thing the seed is about (power, desire, coordination failure, etc.)
- Include domain-crossing hints
- Be specific about constraints
- Make the provocation genuinely uncomfortable

### DON'T:
- Write a product specification (that's the agents' job)
- Limit to a single industry vertical
- Make the provocation answerable
- Provide example ideas (that biases the Generator)
- Be so broad that everything fits
- Skip "The Moment" — it's the anchor that prevents abstraction
